Caffeine is a natural stimulant and is one of the most well-known compounds in coffee. It acts on the central nervous system, promoting alertness and reducing fatigue. The caffeine content in coffee can vary depending on factors like the type of coffee beans, brewing method, and serving size. On average, a typical 8-ounce (237 ml) cup of coffee contains around 95 mg of caffeine, but this can range from 30 to 200 mg or more.

Coffee beans contain chlorogenic acids, which are antioxidants. These compounds have been studied for their potential health benefits, such as reducing inflammation and protecting against certain diseases. The exact content of chlorogenic acids can vary depending on the type of coffee beans and the roasting process.
